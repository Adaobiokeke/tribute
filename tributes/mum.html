A FAREWELL TO MY HUSBAND AND MY FRIEND

Jim dear, it's so sad to come to terms with the fact that you left your beloved children and I without a father and a husband. The entire episode at the hospital seemed so unreal, I had never imagined seeing you go.
The recurring decimal remains "could this be true? Does this mean the roaring of the Lion will, henceforth, cease? Could He have gone with all those endowments? (Still a puzzle).

Our 42years relationship left me with no regrets. You fulfilled every of your wedding vows, made sure I got the best of every good thing you could afford. You were so proactive on my affairs, complimented areas I lacked.
You took time to cultivate me, indeed you were the catalyst for my all-round growth, a huge personality with a heart of gold.
I recall those early days of our relationship, you would cuddle our children while I was far at the other end of the country studying, you never derailed in your responsibility, we enjoyed your company much because you represented warmth and protection.

Your commitment to their studies, especially in mathematics showed up as they all turned to be mathematical geniuses. Your friends too are not left out, they are stunned and saddened by your demise knowing they will miss your unmatchable wisdom, knowledge, courage and oratory. I had often heard your very close friend, Engr. Akinbode Akinluyi, call you severally "the Ahitophel of our time" because of your level of wisdom and decerning ability.

The vacuum in Otiji family and the entire Aguobu-Umumba community is already so glaring but we can only take solace in the noble and enduring legacies you left on the sands of time in your many fields----your career, a responsible family man and by the great impact of your life in your environment.

Lastly, though we no longer enjoy your physical presence in our midst, the ideals for which you lived like a diamond will remain forever.

Remarkably, it is quite interesting to note that you lived long, lived well, richly and splendidly.
Farewell Sweetheart I Miss you so dearly

Grace Oby Otiji
PhD, NAEP, FICA.
