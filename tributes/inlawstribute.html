TRIBUTE TO MY FATHER IN-LAW LATE CHIEF ENGR. JAMES ANIESODO OTIJI

September 19, 2023 was a sad day for me and my family because we lost a caring Father, Grand-Father and Father In-Law to a sudden death which we were never prepared for.
Though we are sad to see you go, we are also privileged to have shared our lives with you.
You have always taught my wife and I how to always love each other, and work together. This lessons we will never forget.
You are also interested in my life endeavors, and has been a positive influence in most of the decisions I have made. I promise you that I will not disappoint you.
I saw how caring and loving you are to my dear wife and my kids, and I appreciate this a lot.
Thank you. You have taught us so many things that we will pass onto generations to come.
Your journey is an inspiration to my family and those whose lives you have touched. You will forever rest in our hearts.
Rest on, Father in-Law, until we meet to part no more.

Engr. Ifeanyi J. Okeke (Son in-law)

THANK YOU

“Thank you “…….Those were your last words to me. Those words also say a lot about our relationship.

As your daughter-in-law, you welcomed me into the family and showered me with so much love and kindness.

You always gave me a reason to say ‘THANKYOU’ .

You were very supportive of me and at every point I needed guidance, you were a voice I could trust.

You always said GOD gave you a special “mindset” and in truth, you had a way of birthing great ideas.

On your last birthday, you said to us that everything you have ever asked God for, he had given it to you, and even more.

Daddy, you lived a fulfilled life and I am grateful to GOD. Thank you for being a great father-in-law.

You will be truly missed but I refuse to grieve like those without hope, but rather thank God for your resurrection and every beautiful year he gave you on earth.

Judith Akunna Otiji
